The Structural Subsidization of Public Health

The cold administrative math of universal healthcare — how co-locating an unregulated parallel tier in public hospitals lets the state claim coverage on paper while shifting the liability onto the patient.

Ch. 02

The cold administrative math of universal healthcare. For a developing state, tertiary ICUs, MD specialists, and modern drug lists are a catastrophic fiscal burden. Legalizing a parallel tier and "co-locating" it in public hospitals lets the state claim high coverage metrics on paper at a fraction of the cost. The argument: institutionalizing alternative medicine is a fiscal-austerity policy disguised as a pluralistic health model.

State healthcare deficit
Legitimize BAMS / AYUSH via policy ↳ lowers infrastructure cost
Co-location in public clinics ↳ claims "universal coverage"
Patient bears biological risk ↳ liability shifts off state
