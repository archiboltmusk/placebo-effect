// the mechanism in one loop

The resurgence of traditional medicine is not a grassroots renaissance of holistic healing. It is a sophisticated, multi-layered collective market machinery — a loop where the state offloads its liability for healthcare, corporations bypass research costs through cultural nationalism, and private trusts monetize an under-trained parallel workforce. The patient bears the entire biological risk.

01 — The State

Offloads its liability for primary healthcare onto an unregulated parallel tier, then counts the coverage on paper.

02 — The Corporation

Bypasses the cost of clinical trials by selling cultural nationalism as a substitute for evidence.

03 — The Trust

Monetizes a desperate, under-trained parallel workforce through private medical colleges.

04 — The Patient

Absorbs the entire biological risk — the only actor in the loop with no exit.

CH.2

The Structural Subsidization of Public Health Outline

fiscal austerity as health policy

The cold administrative math: co-locating an unregulated parallel tier in public hospitals lets the state claim coverage on paper while offloading the liability for care.

Outline ▾

The cold administrative math of universal healthcare. For a developing state, tertiary ICUs, MD specialists, and modern drug lists are a catastrophic fiscal burden. Legalizing a parallel tier and "co-locating" it in public hospitals lets the state claim high coverage metrics on paper at a fraction of the cost. The argument: institutionalizing alternative medicine is a fiscal-austerity policy disguised as a pluralistic health model.

State healthcare deficit
Legitimize BAMS / AYUSH via policy ↳ lowers infrastructure cost
Co-location in public clinics ↳ claims "universal coverage"
Patient bears biological risk ↳ liability shifts off state

CH.3

The Educational Arbitrage Outline

BAMS degrees · NEET bottlenecks

How private trusts monetize the desperation of students locked out of MBBS seats, manufacturing a parallel, under-equipped medical workforce.

Outline ▾

When an MBBS seat is academically or financially out of reach, private trusts capitalize on the desperation created by entrance bottlenecks such as NEET.

  • The Degree Economy. Private BAMS colleges charge high capitation fees to deliver an impossible synthesis — metaphysical concepts (Doshas, Prakriti) bolted onto basic anatomy and physiology.
  • The Career Trap. Legally designated "doctors," structurally unequipped for acute allopathic emergencies, deployed as cheap labor in private ICU night-shifts and rural primary care.

The Mixopathy Crisis Outline

cross-practice · surgical dilution

Outline ▾

The policy shift authorizing alternative-medicine postgraduates to perform specific surgical interventions after short training modules.

// the argument

Surgical competence is not a collection of mechanical tasks; it is a clinical understanding of systemic physiological crises, pharmacological interactions, and emergency resuscitation.

Diluting these standards builds a two-tiered system: the wealthy reach residency-trained surgeons; the rural poor are triaged into subsidized shortcuts. The chapter also examines covert cross-practice — broad-spectrum antibiotics and high-dose corticosteroids prescribed for rapid, deceptive "cures," feeding antimicrobial resistance.
